Recipe for Pounded Yam and White Soup

Ingredients:

For Pounded Yam:
  • Yam (about 1 kg)
  • Water
For White Soup (Ofe Nsala):
  • Goat meat or catfish (about 500g)
  • Cocoyam (about 5 medium-sized corms)
  • Utazi leaves (a handful, thinly sliced)
  • Pepper soup spices (uziza seeds, Ehuru/Ariwo, and Uda)
  • Seasoning cubes or powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Waterleaf or fluted pumpkin leaves (ugu), washed and shredded (substitute spinach if unavailable)
  • Onion (1 medium-sized), chopped
  • Palm oil (optional, for added flavor)
  • Water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Pounded Yam:

    Peel and cut the yam into medium-sized chunks. Rinse thoroughly and place in a pot. Add enough water to cover the yam and boil until tender.

    Once the yam is cooked, drain the water and pound the yam using a mortar and pestle until smooth and stretchy. Add water gradually to achieve the desired consistency.

  2. Prepare White Soup (Ofe Nsala):

    Clean and cut the goat meat or catfish into desired sizes. Peel and cut the cocoyam into small pieces and cook until soft.

    In a separate pot, combine the cooked meat or fish, cooked cocoyam, utazi leaves, pepper soup spices, seasoning cubes, salt, and water. Allow it to simmer until well-cooked and flavors meld.

    Add the shredded waterleaf or fluted pumpkin leaves, chopped onion, and palm oil (if using). Continue cooking until the vegetables are tender.
